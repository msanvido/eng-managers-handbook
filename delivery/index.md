# Delivery

<p class="part-index__lede">You are accountable for what the team ships and when. Not what gets built — that's Product's call — but who builds it, how, and when it lands.</p>

Delivery is the part of the job that's most visible to everyone outside engineering. It's also the part that new managers are most likely to over-index on, because the signals are clear and the feedback is fast. Don't let the visibility mislead you: the work in [Part II — People]({{ '/people/' | relative_url }}) is the one that determines whether this part succeeds.

## What this part covers

Five chapters, each one carved out of the old monolithic "Product" chapter:

1. **[Product and Roadmap Management]({{ '/delivery/product-roadmap.html' | relative_url }})** — the Onion Model, the Who/How/When framework, roadmap levels, and the PM/Engineering/Sales triangle.

2. **[Project Execution]({{ '/delivery/project-execution.html' | relative_url }})** — estimation, tools and repositories, tech debt management, postmortems, decision logs.

3. **[Engineering Operations]({{ '/delivery/eng-operations.html' | relative_url }})** — releases, feature flags, on-call, quality and testing strategy, agile methodology.

4. **[Communication and Delegation]({{ '/delivery/communication-delegation.html' | relative_url }})** — meetings, upward/sideways/downward communication, managing up, delegation levels, decision-making.

5. **[Managing in the AI Era]({{ '/delivery/ai-era.html' | relative_url }})** — what coding agents have changed about staffing, performance evaluation, onboarding, and the junior engineer pipeline. Written for where the industry actually is in 2026, not where it was in 2022.

{: .note }
> Build the product your customers need, not the product you want to build. Stay close to customers, ship frequently, and iterate based on feedback. Perfect is the enemy of shipped.
