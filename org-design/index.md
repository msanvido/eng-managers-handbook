# Org Design

<p class="part-index__lede">Your team structure is your product architecture. Choosing the shape of the team is one of the highest-leverage decisions you make — and one of the easiest to get wrong.</p>

{: .important }
> **Conway's Law** — "organizations design systems that mirror their own communication structure" — is true the first time you read it and truer every time you watch it play out in practice. Your team structure will directly shape your product architecture. Choose deliberately.

This is a standalone chapter, not a sub-topic of people management, because the decisions here are strategic and long-lived. A team restructure you make today will still be visible in your codebase in three years.

[Team Structure and Organization Design]({{ '/org-design/team-structure.html' | relative_url }}) walks through the common models — functional, feature/cross-functional, matrix, Team Topologies, Spotify, DevOps/full-stack — with diagrams, pros and cons, and the conditions under which each one works. It also covers how organizations tend to evolve as they grow, and how to survive a transition between structures without losing people or velocity.
